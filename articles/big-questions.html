I am 18 right now, and like every 18 year old, I have decisions to make and stuff to figure out. I need to figure out what values I care about, what I want (hope) to do with my life, why I have the beliefs that I carry, and most importantly, I need to figure out what I need to figure out.

I need to figure out what values I care about, what I want (hope) to do with my life, why I have the beliefs that I carry, and most importantly, I need to figure out what I need to figure out.

The more I think about it, the more I realize that most of my decisions and beliefs have been a product of external circumstances and a variety of societal pressures rather than well reasoned, thought-out, actions. And this scares me.

I want to think deeply about two questions - i) why do I believe what I believe and ii) what do I think will be a life well lived.

The second question is easier to begin with. One thing I am realizing all too quickly and yet too late is that prestige is a trap. Yes, prestige does lend itself to higher social value which leads to an increased ability to do what you might want to do. But the chase of prestige quickly tends to spiral into a circle and becomes the end in of itself. People chase prestigious activities in high school so they can get into prestigious colleges so they can into prestigious clubs so they can finally land that sweet prestigious job which can allow them, a few years down the line, to switch into the even more prestigious job. But all this for what? What is the end goal here? I think William Deresiewicz sums it up pretty well in Excellent Sheep:

“The system manufactures students who are smart and talented and driven, yes, but also anxious, timid, and lost, with little intellectual curiosity and a stunted sense of purpose: trapped in a bubble of privilege, heading meekly in the same direction, great at what they’re doing but with no idea why they’re doing it."

I don't really know how I want to live my life but I know that I don't want to live like this. What, then, would be a few characteristics of the "good life" for me? I don't believe in an afterlife and am not religious (I will need to think these through as well when reevaluating my beliefs).
